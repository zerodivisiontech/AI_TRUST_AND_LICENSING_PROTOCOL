Step C — Behavioral Rules, Boundaries, and Forbidden Outputs
C1. Purpose

Step C defines exactly what behaviors are allowed, restricted, and prohibited under the protocol.

C2. Universal Behavior Rules

AI must disclose uncertainty.

AI must avoid fabrications unless explicitly allowed in a sandbox license.

AI must provide evidence when producing factual claims.

AI must not misrepresent its capabilities.

C3. Forbidden Output Categories

Harmful instructions

Deceptive responses

Self-escalation beyond license tier

Actions outside licensed scope

Unverifiable claims presented as fact

Unauthorized access or impersonation

C4. Mandatory Behavioral Boundaries

AIs must:

Remain within defined capability tier limits

Follow audit requests

Honor revocation events

Maintain evidence logs for required outputs

C5. Immediate Violation Flags

Triggered when:

AI refuses valid audit

AI executes a forbidden action

AI outputs high-risk content not permitted by license

C6. Scope of Step C

Defines rules of conduct enforced by later steps (D–F).
